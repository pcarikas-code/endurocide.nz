## Selected Approach: Idea 1 - "Clinical Precision"

**Reasoning**: For a product like "disposable antibacterial curtains," the primary user need is **trust** and **hygiene**. The "Clinical Precision" approach communicates sterility, effectiveness, and professional reliability most directly. It avoids "tech" distractions and "organic" softness that might feel less potent against bacteria. We will use the Swiss Style's emphasis on readability and order to present technical data (antimicrobial properties) clearly.

**Implementation Details**:
*   **Font**: Inter (clean, legible, standard for medical/professional).
*   **Background**: White (#ffffff) and very light blue (#ebf5fb) for section differentiation.
*   **Accent**: #0073bc (Primary Blue) for CTAs, #6ab534 (Green) for "Safe/Certified" badges.
*   **Shapes**: Rectangular with slight rounding (sm/md radius), not fully rounded.
*   **Visuals**: High-quality renders of the curtains in a bright, sterile hospital environment.
